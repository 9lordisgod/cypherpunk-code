# Mission & Values

## Our mission

> **Make cypherpunk education accessible, free, and free from trading noise.**

Cypherpunk Code exists to index, organize, and sequence the best freely available material for learning cryptocurrency, privacy technology, and cypherpunk philosophy. We are a **reference archive** — not a course provider, not a trading platform, and not financial advice.

---

## What we believe

### 1. Privacy is a human right

The Cypherpunk Manifesto said it first: privacy is necessary for an open society in the electronic age. We believe education about privacy technology is itself an act of resistance against surveillance capitalism.

### 2. Code is speech

Writing and running code that protects privacy is a form of expression. We index tools and documentation that empower individuals to write their own protections.

### 3. Sovereignty over convenience

Self-custody, self-hosted nodes, and self-directed learning are harder than trusting third parties — and that is the point. We prioritize resources that teach sovereignty, not dependency.

### 4. Education must be free

Knowledge about freedom technology should never be behind a paywall. Every learner, regardless of income or geography, deserves access to high-quality cypherpunk education.

### 5. Signal over noise

The crypto space is flooded with trading content, speculation, and hype. We apply editorial judgment — the Cypherpunk Score — to surface resources about privacy, cryptography, and sovereignty.

### 6. Clarity and independence

We are upfront about what this project is: a curated freedom education index, not a trading platform or certification body. The catalog and documentation are free to browse. We practice what we teach.

## What we cover

- **Bitcoin** — technical foundations, sovereignty, privacy techniques
- **Monero** — private money, node operation, research
- **Cypherpunk history** — manifestos, mailing lists, foundational literature
- **Operational security** — GPG, Tor, Tails, compartmentalization
- **Cryptography** — university-level theory and applied protocol design
- **Privacy tooling** — wallets, CoinJoin, mixers, anonymous networking

---

## What we exclude

- Trading strategies and price analysis
- Speculative altcoin promotion
- Paid course upsells and affiliate funnels
- Content that requires KYC or personal data collection
- Resources that prioritize profit over education

---

## The Cypherpunk Score philosophy

Every resource is rated 1–10 for cypherpunk relevance. This is not a quality rating of the original content — it is a measure of how aligned the resource is with our mission of privacy, sovereignty, and cryptography education.

A Princeton Bitcoin course scores 8. A trading masterclass scores 2. Both might be "good" content in general, but only one belongs in a cypherpunk archive.

## Stewardship

Cypherpunk Code is curated and maintained by [@CHxmrBrother](https://x.com/CHxmrBrother).

The initial database was seeded from public sources including:

- Cypherpunk School
- Nakamoto Institute
- Monero Project documentation
- MIT OpenCourseWare
- Princeton Coursera courses
- Class Central aggregations
- Community guides and wikis

New resources are added through curator review and community suggestions. If any rights holder is unhappy with a listing, contact us — content will be reviewed and removed promptly.
